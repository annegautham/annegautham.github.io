Under Professor Ramana Davuluri, I applied NLP methods (Transformers, DNABERT) to biological systems. DNABERT is a pre-trained Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers model for DNA-language in genomes. 

<br>

DNABERT attempts to tackle sequence specificity prediction with a "top-down" approach by developing a general understanding of the DNA language via a self-supervised pre-training and applying itself to specific tasks, such as predicting promotores or transcription factor binding sites and splice sites. It is expected that the pre-trained DNABERT on the human genome can be readily applied to data from other organisms with exceptional performance. 

<br>

Specifically, I studied tissue specificity as markers in Acute Myeloid Leukemia by applying DNABERT to undersand TSp promoters and determine which transcript variants affect the cancer and how.
